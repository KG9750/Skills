# City Hunter Technique Notes

Use this reference only when the user asks for City Hunter-related analysis, technique grounding, or source-backed style constraints. Convert all observations into original, non-infringing craft guidance.

## Source Baseline

- City Hunter is a manga by Tsukasa Hojo, serialized in Weekly Shonen Jump from 1985 to 1991. The original Jump Comics release ran 35 volumes; Shueisha's bunko edition ran 18 volumes.
- Public descriptions commonly classify the work as urban action, comedy, and detective/commission-driven crime fiction.
- Official modern materials continue to frame the series around a Shinjuku-based underground problem-solver, city crime, gun action, adult charisma, and comedy partnership dynamics.

## Transferable Technique Summary

### Paneling

- Treat a manga panel as the most decisive instant from a sequence, not a frame-by-frame transcript.
- Establish space before emotion: city layout, distance between people, line of sight, props, and exits should be clear before close-ups carry tension.
- Use wide city panels for pressure, mid-shots for relationship geometry, and close-ups for decision, suspicion, attraction, embarrassment, or punchline release.
- In action scenes, preserve cause and effect: target, obstacle, tactical choice, impact, and changed scene status.

### Composition

- Let urban realism carry tone. Streets, station exits, cafes, office towers, alleys, rooftops, glass, concrete, traffic, rain, and signs should be story machinery, not wallpaper.
- Separate serious and comic space. Serious scenes use stable perspective, readable anatomy, detailed backgrounds, and heavier shadows. Comic beats can flatten space, simplify faces, and exaggerate posture.
- Keep props legible. Weapons, vehicles, phones, coats, bags, cigarettes, glasses, and doorways should clarify role and intent.

### Character Design

- Build adult characters from posture, job, clothing, and prop logic before surface style.
- Give each lead three linked states:
  - Professional: competent silhouette, controlled expression, functional clothing.
  - Private: relaxed posture, quieter eyes, softer body language.
  - Comic break: simplified face, elastic body language, overconfident pose collapse.
- Keep silhouette anchors consistent across all states so the reader recognizes the character after exaggeration.

### Rhythm

- The core timing pattern is pressure build, comic rupture, fast recovery to the case.
- Comedy should reveal character flaws or relationship tension, not pause the story without consequence.
- For contemporary work, replace dated harassment-based jokes with consent-aware ego punctures, social misreads, overconfidence, status loss, or slapstick consequences.

### Urban Story Engine

- Give every recurring location a function:
  - Commission point: where clients enter the story.
  - Information point: where rumors, surveillance, or deals surface.
  - Danger point: where ambush or pursuit is likely.
  - Retreat point: where characters regroup.
  - Quiet point: where the emotional truth can surface.
- A case should move characters through these points while changing distance, trust, and danger.

## Prompt Conversion Rules

Allowed phrasing:

- retro 1980s-1990s urban action manga
- cinematic manga paneling
- realistic city backgrounds
- hardboiled action-comedy timing
- adult-proportion character design
- clear ink linework with dense urban texture
- expressive comic deformation during punchline beats

Disallowed phrasing:

- in the style of Tsukasa Hojo
- 北条司风格
- City Hunter character names or direct equivalents
- exact franchise props, catchphrases, message codes, costumes, or scene setups
- copied panel layout from a manga page

## Output Check

Before delivering generated material, verify:

- The story, characters, city, and case are original.
- The prompt does not name a living artist as the style target.
- No recognizable City Hunter character, panel, catchphrase, or signature setup appears.
- Comedy does not depend on harassment or coercive romantic behavior.
- Action can be followed from geography and cause-effect, not just speed lines.
