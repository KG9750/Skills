# Retro Urban Action Manga

## Purpose

Use this skill to build original urban action-comedy manga material with a late-1980s/early-1990s mood: realistic city pressure, adult character silhouettes, clear action causality, detective-style commissions, and abrupt comedy reversals.

This skill can reference `references/city-hunter-technique-notes.md` for background and technique notes. Load that file when the user asks specifically about City Hunter, Tsukasa Hojo, technique analysis, source-backed notes, or style constraints.

## Safety and Style Boundary

- Do not produce prompts that ask for work "in the style of Tsukasa Hojo", "北条司风格", or equivalent living-artist imitation.
- Do not recreate City Hunter characters, exact story devices, slogans, panel layouts, dialogue, costumes, or identifiable scenes.
- Translate influence into generic craft terms: retro urban action manga, cinematic paneling, realistic city backgrounds, adult-proportion character design, hardboiled comedy timing.
- Keep any fan-work or analysis clearly labeled as analysis. For new work, create original characters, locations, cases, and visual motifs.
- Modernize dated sexual-comedy dynamics. Preserve "ego puncture" and comic comeuppance timing without normalizing harassment.

## Intake

When details are missing, infer conservatively or ask only for high-impact choices:

- Story basis: original pitch, prose scene, case outline, or visual brief.
- Format: page-based manga, vertical webtoon, storyboard table, or visual bible.
- Scope: page count, panel count, chapter beat, or character/location set.
- Era and city: exact city if provided; otherwise use a fictional dense nightlife district rather than a real franchise-specific location.
- Mode: black-and-white manga, limited-color cover, animation pitch boards, or image-generation prompts.
- Rating and comedy limit: action intensity, romance level, and what kinds of jokes are out of scope.

## Technique Pass

Before writing panels or prompts, define the craft spine:

1. **City function**: give each location a job, such as commission entry point, information node, surveillance perch, chase corridor, safe room, or emotional reset space.
2. **Action causality**: every action sequence needs a target, obstacle, body/weapon decision, visible consequence, and changed power relation.
3. **Distance map**: track who is close, who is separated, who has line of sight, and what prop or threshold changes the scene.
4. **Tone switch**: place comedy after pressure has accumulated, then return quickly to the case objective.
5. **Character states**: design each lead in serious/professional, private/vulnerable, and exaggerated comic states while keeping the same silhouette anchors.

## Storyboard Output

For storyboards, output a compact page/panel table. Include:

- Page and panel number.
- Shot size and angle.
- Composition and city/background role.
- Character action and action causality.
- Dialogue function, not just dialogue text.
- Comedy beat or emotional turn, if present.
- Continuity note for props, distance, wounds, vehicles, lighting, or eye lines.

Default page rhythm:

- Establish the city pressure with a wide or tall panel.
- Move into mid-shots that clarify relationships and distance.
- Use close-ups for decision, desire, suspicion, or joke release.
- Reserve splash or near-splash panels for irreversible action or emotional revelation.

## Visual Bible Output

For visual bibles, include:

- **Premise line**: original case engine and tone.
- **Character triads**: serious state, private state, comic break state.
- **Silhouette anchors**: hair mass, shoulder line, coat/jacket shape, posture, signature prop category.
- **Urban palette and materials**: asphalt, glass, neon, train platforms, office corridors, rain, smoke, concrete, metal railings.
- **Line and shadow rules**: cleaner face contours, denser background texture, strong cast shadows, selective speed lines, readable weapon/vehicle silhouettes.
- **Location map**: commission point, information point, danger point, retreat point, emotional quiet point.
- **Prompt rules**: image prompts may use era, medium, lighting, lens, and manga craft terms, but not living-artist names or franchise-specific identifiers.

## Image Prompt Pattern

Use this structure for original image prompts:

```text
Original retro 1980s-1990s urban action manga panel, [shot size and angle],
[fictional city location and function], [original character description],
[specific action with cause and consequence], cinematic paneling,
realistic city background detail, expressive but non-franchise character design,
[black-and-white ink / limited color / screentone], [lighting and mood].
Negative: living artist imitation, franchise characters, recognizable City Hunter scene,
exact copied panel layout, logo, watermark.
```
